# Visual Argument Map

This note explains why Figures 9, 12, and 13 are no longer generic instrumentation summaries.
They are run-specific visual arguments.

Supporting figure docs:

- [Synthetic Figures 9 / 12 / 13](paper_synthetic_figures_09_12_13.md)
- [NASA Milling Figures 9 / 12 / 13](paper_milling_figures_09_12_13.md)
- [NASA Bearings Figures 9 / 12 / 13](paper_bearings_figures_09_12_13.md)

## Figure 9 Argument

Figure 9 is the structural-separation argument:

- same or very similar primary behavior
- different higher-order or meta-residual structure
- different grammar / detectability / downstream outcome

What it changes:

- a reviewer can no longer dismiss the engine as “just another magnitude alarm”
- an operator can see why first-order residual magnitude alone is insufficient in the current run

## Figure 12 Argument

Figure 12 is the semantic-process argument:

- semantic interpretation unfolds through time
- ambiguity can narrow without being faked into certainty
- disposition and candidate-set evolution matter more than a single final label

What it changes:

- the retrieval layer is legible as a process, not a static bank-existence statement
- a reviewer can inspect when interpretation stabilizes and when it remains conservative

## Figure 13 Argument

Figure 13 is the interpretability-delta argument:

- baseline comparator view shows threshold/alarm behavior
- DSFB adds grammar, structure, and semantic timeline context
- the point is interpretability delta, not superiority bragging

What it changes:

- an engineer can see what the baseline already catches
- the figure then shows what DSFB adds without claiming the baseline is obsolete

## Why These Figures Matter

These figures change understanding in a way generic instrumentation does not:

- Figure 9 supports separation decisions when primary behavior looks deceptively similar
- Figure 12 supports interpretation-confidence decisions over time
- Figure 13 supports monitor-stack integration decisions by making the DSFB delta explicit

All three datasets now support this argument in their own run-specific way:

- synthetic for controlled textbook separation
- NASA milling for process-window structure
- NASA bearings for failure-progression structure
